Crispy Fried Shrimp

Crispy fried shrimp is such a delightful treat! The golden, crunchy coating paired with tender shrimp makes for a dish that’s simply irresistible. It’s perfect for a cozy meal or a fun family dinner. Let’s make this together and enjoy every bite!
Ingredients
  • 1 pound shrimp, peeled and deveined

  • 1 cup all-purpose flour

  • 1 cup breadcrumbs (panko for extra crunch)

  • 2 eggs, beaten
  • 1 teaspoon garlic powder

  • 1 teaspoon paprika

  • Salt and pepper to taste

  • Vegetable oil (for frying)
  • Lemon wedges (for serving)

Instruction


  1. Prepare the Shrimp - Rinse the shrimp under cold water and pat them dry with paper towels. This helps the coating stick better.


  2. Set Up Breading Stations - In one bowl, mix the flour with garlic powder, paprika, salt, and pepper.In another bowl, place the beaten eggs.In a third bowl, add the breadcrumbs.


  3. Bread the Shrimp - Dip each shrimp first into the flour mixture, making sure it’s well-coated.Next, dip it into the beaten eggs, allowing any excess to drip off.Finally, roll the shrimp in the breadcrumbs until fully coated..


  4. Heat the Oil - In a large skillet or deep fryer, heat about 1 inch of vegetable oil over medium-high heat until hot (about 350°F or 175°C).


  5. Fry the Shrimp - Carefully add the breaded shrimp to the hot oil in batches, making sure not to overcrowd the pan.Fry for 2-3 minutes on each side, or until golden brown and crispy. Use a slotted spoon to remove them from the oil and place them on a paper towel-lined plate to drain excess oil.


Extra Knowledge
Did you know that shrimp is not only tasty but also low in calories while being high in protein? It also contains important nutrients like selenium and vitamin B12. Making fried shrimp at home means you can control the ingredients and ensure they’re fresh and delicious!
Benifit and disadvantages

Crispy fried shrimp can be a fun source of protein and is loved by many for its crunch and flavor. However, frying adds extra calories and fat, so it’s best to enjoy this dish occasionally as part of a balanced diet.
